Overview
About This Project
This is the design research behind "Chdata on OSRS PvP in 2026" — a longform YouTube video pitching concrete improvements to the Wilderness. The three core problems addressed are: risk not being worth the reward, the skill gap being too large for new entrants, and a polling doom spiral where demographic shifts make passing PvP updates progressively harder over time. The pages below represent each major proposal or supporting analysis, styled in the OSRS aesthetic using the RuneScape chat font.

Core Proposal
Blessed Status
An opt-in overhead status for the Wilderness — the counterpart to Skulling. Spend 100k GP to keep your items on death, with a 5% fee paid to the PKer. Directly addresses risk not being worth the reward.

Core Proposal
Wilderness Teams
An official small-group party system for the Wilderness. Up to 6 players with anti-friendly-fire, a recruitment board, and enforced trust between teammates. Addresses the skill-gap and solo-unfriendly multi-combat problem.

Forinthry Castle
Deep Wilderness clan siege content. A structured objective-based PvP zone in the deep Wildy designed to give large groups a reason to fight over territory with meaningful rewards and escalating stakes.

Extended Proposal
Colosseum of Zeah
A 6v6 structured PvP arena minigame. Tiered reward shops, season modifiers, ELO matchmaking, and anti-abuse measures. Designed to onboard players into PvP without the chaos of the open Wilderness.

Mechanic
Rag Skull
An anti-rag mechanic proposal. When a skulled player attacks another skulled player in low-value gear, a Rag Skull is applied with modified drop and run behavior. Targets the ragging problem without blanket restrictions.
