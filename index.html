About This Project
This is the design research behind "Chdata on OSRS PvP in 2026", a longform YouTube video pitching concrete improvements to the Wilderness. The pages below represent each major proposal or supporting analysis.
Analysis
The Core Problems of PvP
PvP is like a Fishing Trawler, and it's full of holes. Fixing just one or two problems while leaving the others means the ship is still sinking. For example even if we fix the skill gap, players not liking risk is still a problem. Even if we fix those, the polling system is still a problem. Even if we fix all of that, that doesn't guarantee that the actual design of specific activities is any good. Every problem below is a separate limiting factor on the same thing: the growth of the PvP playerbase. The three biggest problems are listed first.
#1
Risk is Not Worth the Reward
This is one of the largest single barriers to entry. Most players will never engage with the Wilderness because they don't want to risk losing their items. And those who might engage are turned away because the risk-to-reward ratio is simply not competitive with the rest of the game. Even safe PvP minigames are not rewarding enough for your time spent.
► Show related problems
  • ►Wilderness rewards have to compete with the rest of the game, including 18m/h from bosses like the Doom of Mokhaiotl - a value that no Wilderness activity can realistically match without creating other integrity problems
  • ►PKing is a negative-sum game due to the cost of supplies (food, potions, runes, ammo) and degrading equipment charges lost by both players every fight.
  • ►The risk of being killed after farming for an hour means a player can end a session with less GP than they started with, while a non-Wildy player in the same time would always profit
  • ►Content like the Wildy Altar fills your inventory with bones, leaving you at an inherent gear and supply disadvantage even if you wanted to PvP while doing that
  • ►Anti-PKing while doing Wilderness bosses means your gear is optimized for the boss, not for player combat - a commonly debated gear disadvantage. You have to bring 3-way tribridding switches to be competitive, which means you have to risk more, which brings us back to the problem of players not wanting to risk
  • ►A large majority of the playerbase is genuinely risk-averse in a way that can't be solved by just raising the rewards - plenty of players will never take any level of GP risk in PvP
  • ►Ironmen lose too much on death in the Wilderness but also gain absolutely nothing from killing other players, making most Wilderness content feel like dead content for a growing share of the votable playerbase
  • ►Safe PvP minigames share the same reward-competition problem - even with zero risk, their rewards don't compete with normal PvM content, so players don't engage
  • ►Arbitrary costs gatekeep basic PKing infrastructure: 1m to unlock loot keys, 5m for nearby respawn points at Edgeville or Ferox Enclave, years of Trouver parchment problems
  • ►Many players, specifically ironmen, are forced into the Wilderness if they wish to obtain items usable outside it (Voidwaker, Mage Arena II capes, previously Dragon Pickaxes), generating hostility between PvP and non-PvP players that is not mutual interest
#2
The Skill Gap is Too Large
New players are fighting veterans the moment they step foot into the Wilderness, and pretty much any PvP activity for that matter. The skill floor is too high, the skill ceiling is even higher with NH bridding, and there is no understood progression ladder. Unlike PvM - which has content for every skill level - PvP has almost nothing literally designed for casual or new players.
► Show related problems
  • ►It costs too much to even try out Wilderness PvP due to risk - the financial entry cost alone discourages experimentation before the skill gap is even a factor, especially more so for newer players who don't have a steady way to earn gold yet
  • ►There is no obvious skill ladder, no understood progression system, and no clear path for bridging the gap between a new player and a competent PKer
  • ►LMS gives players free gear but the skill gap problem persists inside LMS itself - equalized levels and gear just makes the mechanical skill gap more visible, not less
  • ►The PvP Arena's meta quickly devolved into players instantly forfeiting for the reward points, bypassing any meaningful competitive play where one could potentially learn
  • ►Casual PvP is essentially dead - low-stakes PvP venues that once kept the skill pipeline healthy (Castle Wars, Soul Wars) are no longer active enough to actually train in
  • ►The Wilderness functions differently from LMS and the PvP Arena, meaning those activities only partially teach transferable skills. Multi combat, world dynamics, chasing and escape gameplay, good gear setups for the Wilderness, are all untouched
  • ►There is no safe PvP minigame venue for "honor PvP" - aka what Bounty Hunter does where it has rules that promote Edge-style Venge PvP with no protection prayers and no magical freezes
  • ►Gear disparity compounds the skill gap - players in max gear fighting players in budget gear creates a compounding disadvantage that is hard to distinguish from being outplayed. This is especially prominent in Castle Wars and Soul Wars.
  • ►The PvP population is declining, not growing alongside the OSRS population - new players are not being converted into PvPers at any meaningful rate. We should be expecting PvP to grow as OSRS itself gets more players, not diminish. This is a LARGELY alarming sign to me about the state of PvP as a whole
  • ►The OSRS PvP wiki could probably do with better information and findable pages about PvP, especially because it's probably most player's biggest resources for learning about the game
#3
The Self-Defeating PvP Polls
PvP needs updates to improve. But because PvP has a terrible reputation, most players vote no on PvP updates. So they don't pass. So PvP never improves. So the reputation stays bad. Jagex, seeing the pattern, reduces development resources on PvP. The cycle feeds itself.
► Show related problems
  • ►PvP updates have consistently failed to pass 70% polls, so Jagex deprioritizes development resources for PvP, making future updates even less likely
  • ►The 70% poll threshold is often antithetical to PvP game balance - 90% of players not engaging in PvP voting on PvP balance changes is a self-fulfilling prophecy that says PvP polls will rarely ever pass because it's not in the interests of 90% of the community
  • ►Likewise, if only PKers are asked to design the Wilderness, they will create a Wilderness that is great for them, but extremely adverse to PvMers, thus also limiting potential growth of PvP as a whole. What is truly needed are compromises between both sides
  • ►It's almost a given that any update that is meant to be a 50/50 fair split in how it affects PvPers and PvMers, is something that can only appeal to 50% of players rather than 70%. Likewise if any update favors PvPers 100% more, well it's not going to pass a 70% threshold from the FULL community
  • ►The negative reputation of PvP is self-perpetuating - if everyone tells new players the Wilderness sucks, new players never try it, so the community stays small and stagnates
  • ►An "us vs them" mentality between PvMers and PvPers means both sides treat polls as a fight to win rather than a system for finding compromise that works for the whole community
  • ►PvPers rarely ask "what would make non-PvPers or "fence sitters" actually enjoy PvP?" - instead defaulting to "just try it" or "you don't get it", which does not bring new players in
  • ►PvMers who say "just remove PvP" are offering an amputation, not a cure - and they underestimate how much of OSRS's identity is tied to the Wilderness existing
  • ►Jagex has proposed the same category of solutions (new content, new rewards, new bosses) for 10+ years without addressing actual core problems - so the community has learned to distrust PvP updates before they even launch
  • ►Ironmen represent a large and growing share of the playerbase. If PvP updates are universally seen as dead content for ironmen, that's a near-automatic bloc of no votes against anything PvP-related. And many players have estimated that ironmen are over 30% of the population, which means almost automatic poll failures for PvP
  • ►The only way Jagex seriously considers a PvP update is when there's a major community outcry - meaning reactive development rather than proactive design is the default mode
🗡
Predator-Prey Design Problems
The core loop of the Wilderness - PKers hunting players doing content - creates structural frustration for both sides, and the food chain that's supposed to generate healthy activity isn't producing the population flow it needs to function.
► Show problems
  • ►PvMers feel constantly interrupted and hunted even when just trying to complete content - world-hopping scouts and clans mean there's no quiet moment
  • ►PKers simultaneously feel the Wilderness is empty - they hop 50 to 300 worlds to find one player risking almost nothing
  • ►PKers killing clue scroll hunters for minimal GP reward is anti-fun for both parties - it wastes everyone's time and resources without meaningful interaction
  • ►The predator-prey model has not been rejuvenating the Wilderness for 13+ years - luring PvMers in with better rewards has never converted them into PvPers
  • ►There are no PvM bosses, monsters, dungeons, or skilling resources that can by themselves revive the Wilderness - that approach has been tried repeatedly and has not worked
  • ►Multi Rev Caves brought significant activity but also brought serious integrity problems (botting, scouting, clans monopolizing, rwt, gold inflation) that are difficult to manage at scale. PKers will forgive these problems, but potential new PvPers are not sold that these are not problems
  • ►World-hopping and logging out is mandatory optimal gameplay rather than actually roaming through the Wilderness. Freeze logging is the most prominent escape strategy instead of actually gearing up to survive running away. Plugins like Wilderness Player Alarm bring frustrations about making it too easy for players to escape without even having to pay attention.
  • ►As OSRS adds more game worlds to handle general population growth, the Wilderness spreads thinner - more worlds, same (declining) PvP players, less activity per world
  • ►The Wilderness has no stated design goal - players, PKers, and Jagex all assign different implicit labels to it, making it impossible to evaluate whether it's succeeding at anything
⚠
Integrity & Cheating
The PvP environment has a serious fairness problem. Cheating tools are widespread and Jagex's response has been inconsistent. When a bot ban wave cleared the Wilderness, it revealed just how little legitimate PvP was actually happening. I think the biggest problem here is lack of communication that Jagex actually cares to take a stance on some of these problems and say they are taking action, such as when Wildy CCTV existed for around a year.
► Show problems
  • ►Cheat clients that auto-pray and auto-eat remove a large part of the mechanical skill from PvP, giving a significant competitive advantage over legitimate players
  • ►AHK and similar scripts that perform instant weapon switches automatically reduce a high-skill-ceiling mechanic to a zero-effort action
  • ►Bots farming GP in the Wilderness suppress organic activity and distort the population numbers Jagex uses to evaluate PvP health. To be honest, it's a little goofy that PKers often recommend people use "killing bots" as an introductory way to engage with the Wilderness
  • ►World-hop scouting bots and tools like WildyCCTV (when it existed) let players see Wilderness activity across all worlds, giving a systematic information advantage to those willing to use them
  • ►Alt account scouting is abused by pretty much everyone, and I think it's a symptom of how empty the Wilderness is. People couldn't be doing that if there were so much activity, their alts were regularly being killed. The emptiness of the Wilderness allows for these kinds of loopholes that both PvMers and PvPers detest more than if they were just organically running into each other
  • ►The wilderness player alarm Runelite plugin allows PvMers to instantly log out when another player appears, while barely having to pay attention at all to the game, make it that much more impossible for a PKer to actually catch them. Most importantly, it's just inorganic gameplay that wasn't always in the game, was not polled for acceptance, and was created by a third party.
  • ►Ragging - repeatedly attacking a player with low-risk gear to waste their supplies or get a lucky kill on them - has no good systemic counter in the current game
  • ►Many Wilderness reward sources have low combat requirements but high GP yields, making them attractive botting targets and an ongoing source of economy problems. Even singles revs was just full of bots before the ban waves. Even PKers were making tutorials about how to farm killing the BOTS
⚙
PvM vs PvP Mechanical Inconsistencies
A significant number of items, staves, and mechanics work differently in PvP versus PvM, or in the Wilderness versus the rest of the game. This creates a hidden layer of knowledge new players don't know to look for that even confuses some veteran players.
► Show problems
  • ►The elemental tomes (fire, water, earth) have wildly different stats in PvM and PvP
  • ►Autocasting on staves behaves differently between PvP and PvM contexts, creating confusion for players transitioning between modes. It's not just inconsistent between PvM and PvP. It's inconsistent between PvP and PvP. Autocast works different in the Wilderness, in Castle Wars, in Soul Wars, in the PvP Arena, in Bounty Hunter, and so on and so forth. It's a core game mechanic and even some of the developers at Jagex have acknowledged they'd rather clear this one up. You can find quotes in the official OSRS PvP Feedback Discord.
  • ►Most powered staves are disabled in PvP, which is counterintuitive and limits the viability of several otherwise useful magic weapons.
  • ►Spellbook and prayer icon resizing is disabled in PvP - a small but representative example of many differences that accumulate into a confusing and annoying experience. Players cannot comfortably use the interface setups they are used to, and are instead forced to play with a generalized setup should they choose to engage with the content at all.
  • ►Boxing - repeatedly attacking a player with weak equipment to prevent anyone else from attacking - is an exploit-adjacent loophole in combat mechanics
+
Other Problems
Additional specific problems that don't fit neatly under one of the above categories, but are each a real hole in the ship.
► Show problems
  • ►The PvP Arena has its own separate set of problems and is effectively non-functional as a competitive venue or activity at all in its current state
  • ►The content gap: almost all PvP content is designed around high-skill solo NH bridding or large clan warfare, leaving a near-total gap for small teams, risk-averse players, or forms of PvP that are generally easier for players to engage with, like Honor Fights.
  • ►Deathdotting - standing on the exact same tile as another player after freezing them to make targeting difficult - is a widespread nuisance technique that many players dislike. Many players also do like it. This is another example of content gap. Something like a safe version of Bounty Hunter would address this problem as a way players can engage with PvP without dealing with a mechanic they don't like, without having to change the rest of PvP in the game
  • ►PJ Timer mechanics are killed off singles teams, leaving no place in the game for them to enjoy PvP
  • ►Various Wilderness hotspots can be monopolized by organized clans, making the Wilderness functionally inaccessible to solo players or small groups
  • ►There is a measurable perception gap between how PvMers and PKers each estimate "risk" - they are not having the same conversation because they are not talking about the same numbers or situations
  • ►Mobile players have a meaningfully different and underaddressed experience - the interface and controls for PvP on mobile are not well-suited to the speed and complexity of OSRS PvP

Wilderness Solution Proposals
Main Design Documents
Given all of this information, I've come up with 4 major systems to help improve the Wilderness and turn a dying activity into a growing activity.
Core Proposal
Blessed Status
An opt-in overhead status for the Wilderness. It's an alternative option to Skulling. Spend 100k GP to keep your items on death, with a 5% fee paid to the PKer who kills you. Directly addresses risk not being worth the reward and skill gap problems. This is the single most impactful possible update Jagex could make to the Wilderness.
View document ►
Core Proposal
Wildy Teams
An official small-group party system for the Wilderness. Up to 6 players with anti-friendly-fire and a cross-world recruitment board in Edgeville. Addresses risk, the skill-gap, and solo-unfriendly multi-combat problem.
View document ►
Core Proposal
Team Combat Zones
A third Wilderness combat zone between singles and full multi, where any one "team" can be attacked by at most 6 players at a time. AKA the "limited multi" concept. It gives Jagex a new way to balance content in the Wilderness that brings back a style of play we lost after the PJ timer changes - small teams gameplay.
View document ►

Additional PvP Concepts
Bonus Design Documents
I have various other ideas that I think would be great for PvP. But I think these are a lower priority to the main big 4 above. In some cases, some of these ideas require something from the big 4 to be implemented first, or success of the big 4 obsoletes some of these ideas.
Core Proposal
Forinthry Castle
Deep Wilderness clan siege content. A structured objective-based PvP zone in the Deepest Wilderness designed to give large groups a reason to fight over territory with meaningful rewards. It requires the implementation of Blessed Status, and it's riding off of the old version of the community-created Wilderness Expansion concept. We sure as hell are not getting a Wilderness Expansion when PvP is in such a terrible spot. Fixing that precedes this.
View document ►
Extended Proposal
Forinthry Gates & Artefacts
A supplementary proposal that builds on Blessed Status. Forinthry Gates let non-Blessed players consolidate their Wilderness PvM earnings into protectable artefacts without leaving the Wilderness — insuring loot you've earned rather than risking all of it on the run back to the bank. Requires Blessed Status to already be in the game.
View document ►
Extended Proposal
6v6 PvP Arena
A 6v6 structured PvP arena minigame. Tiered reward shops, season modifiers, ELO matchmaking, and anti-bot-abuse measures. A fun PvP minigame in its own right, but also designed to onboard players into PvP without the chaos of the open Wilderness. I would love to see a minigame like this some day, but if we can't fix our current PvP first, why would we make a new PvP minigame?
View document ►
Mechanic
Rag Skull
An anti-ragging mechanic proposal. When a player skulls against another player, if the initiator is not risking enough, a Rag Skull is applied which acts identical to a High Risk Skull. Targets the ragging problem without the need for manual case-by-case moderation by Jagex. This is something that I think would be great for Jagex to add right now. Both PvMers and PvPers I've talked to seem to universally hate ragging. This is the best way to diminish it.
View document ►
Mechanic
Removal of Trouver Parchments
Jagex already said they want to remove Trouver Parchments — the blocker is GP generation. This proposal removes that blocker entirely: untradeable items drop as tradeable broken or mangled shards on death, giving the killer something to sell and the owner something to buy back. No raw GP enters the game at any point.
View document ►
UI Mockup
Wilderness Activity Listing
A mockup of the in-game world hop list extended with a Wilderness activity flame indicator. Players can see at a glance which worlds have active PvP before hopping. It's a better solution than reducing the Wilderness to a limited number of worlds. Theoretically, if the Wilderness just becomes more active on ALL worlds, which is the goal of my big 4 solutions, then this shouldn't be necessary at all.

Other Players' Suggestions
Ideas Worth Signal Boosting
These are not my original ideas, but they are ideas I think deserve more attention. Some of them have been floating around the community for years. All of them would materially improve the Wilderness.
Signal Boost
Ironman Loot Keys to Bond Coffers
Let ironmen convert Wilderness Loot Keys into a Bond-only Coffer. Ironmen make up 30% of the playerbase and currently have zero mechanical reason to PK — or to vote yes on PvP polls. This closes both problems at once, especially when paired with Blessed Status.
View document ►
Signal Boost
Wilderness-Only Clue Scrolls
A dedicated clue scroll tier for the Wilderness, obtained from Wilderness content and completed entirely within it. Removes forced Wilderness steps from standard clues, and replaces them with opt-in activity that actually fills the Wilderness with engaged, roaming players. +6,300 upvotes on Reddit — five years ago.
