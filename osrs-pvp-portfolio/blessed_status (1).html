Blessed Status

A new opt-in overhead status for the Wilderness — an alternative to Skulling. Keep your items on death, for a fee.

What Is It?
Blessed Status is PvM deaths in the Wilderness — except the item reclamation fee is paid to the PKer who killed you, and it costs a minimum risk of 100k to enable.

Skulling makes you lose everything on death. Blessed Status lets you keep everything — but you pay a 5% death fee proportional to the gear value you brought into the Wilderness, and a 25% death fee to keep any items you obtain within the Wilderness. It is the opposite of Skulled Status, and has a Saradomin symbol overhead — just as Skulling is a symbol of Zamorak.

Blessed Status is enabled and disabled at NPC monks near Wilderness entry points (Wildy Ditch, Ferox Enclave, Mage Arena). Costs 100k each activation. Leaving the Wilderness temporarily disables it until you re-enter. Dying removes it, requiring it to be activated again.

Key Rules
The mechanics that make it work
  • Blessed Status is toggled from NPC monks at Wilderness entry points — the Wildy Ditch, Ferox Enclave, and Mage Arena. Costs 100k to enable. You can only toggle it off at those same NPCs, which refunds the 100k.
  • Leaving the Wilderness deactivates Blessed Status. Re-entering reactivates it automatically until you toggle it off.
  • Dying removes Blessed Status. You pay 5% GE value of all items you are carrying as a reclamation fee — paid first from your 100k deposit, then from death coffers and your bank.
  • If your reclamation fee is less than 100k, the remainder of the 100k goes to whoever killed you — making 100k the minimum risk and preventing fee-free ragging as a Blessed player.
  • Items gained inside the Wilderness from PvM or skilling after entering incur an additional 25% fee to reclaim.
  • The full fee (5% and 25%) is delivered to whoever killed you via a Blessed Key — redeemable at any bank, Wilderness loot chest, or the NPC monks. If no player killed you, the fee is sunk from the game.
  • Blessed Status overrides any Skull you carry when entering the Wilderness. Wearing a Cape of Skulls or Amulet of Avarice cannot skull you while Blessed. Even if an edge case in multi allowed an attack that would normally skull you, the Blessed Status cannot be overridden.
  • Blessed players are locked into Skull Prevention — they cannot initiate attacks that would skull them, and cannot join a pile on a skulled or 3-iteming player who is already being attacked unless that player attacks them first.
  • Blessed players can freely fight other Blessed players — both are sent to Death's Office on death regardless of outcome.
  • Attacking a Blessed player does not skull the attacker.
  • Wearing the Amulet of Avarice is incompatible with Blessed Status — it is unequippable while Blessed, and cannot provide noted drops from Revenants.
  • The Protect Item prayer negates the fee on your single most expensive item.
  • Higher value gear may incur a scaling percentage fee — discouraging fully maxed BiS setups under Blessed Status without outright blocking them.
  • At Wilderness level 10 or below, the fee is reduced to 1% to encourage Edgeville activity.
  • A toggleable warning tells you on entry whether you are entering with Blessed Status active, and displays your estimated death fee based on your current equipped gear. You can also check it in the Items Lost on Death menu.
No safe zone anywhere. Blessed Status does not make you invincible or immune to attack. You can still be killed. You simply lose a fee rather than your items.
Tiered Blessed Status
Stay in the Wilderness longer — earn greater rewards

By completing Wilderness activities without leaving or dying, Blessed players accumulate Tiers that increase loot and add bonuses — rewarding sustained engagement over constant worldhopping.

Tiers are earned by accumulating stars through Wilderness activity. Each method below takes roughly an hour to complete all five stars via that path.

  • Boss kills: Kill a Wilderness boss 1, 2, 4, 8, then 16 times (31 kills total) to earn stars 1–5.
  • Looting: Accumulate 100k, 200k, 300k, 400k, and 500k in GP value from monsters or chests (1.5m total) to earn stars 1–5. A single drop worth 1.5m or more from any Wilderness monster instantly jumps you to all 5 stars.
  • Agility: Complete 15, 30, 60, 90, then 120 laps of the Wilderness Agility Course (315 laps total) to earn stars 1–5.
  • PKing: Kill a player to steal all of their stars.
  • Logout or world-hop: Lose 1 star.
  • Die or leave the Wilderness entirely: Your tier resets to 0. Entering a safe zone such as the Mage Bank or Ferox Enclave also resets to 0.
  • Forinthry Gates allow Tiered Blessed players to log out or world-hop without losing any tiers — rewarding players who stay in one world rather than hopping constantly.
Ring of Greed
High risk, high reward — for players who want to go all in

If Blessed Status were implemented, it would naturally follow that Skulling should still be the better money-making method. Blessed Status starts at -25% loot and caps out at +75% loot. Skulled players, by contrast, keep the full risk-reward ratio the Wilderness already provides — and that gap is intentional.

We already see this pattern with the Amulet of Avarice, which gives Revenant noted drops and a drop rate bonus, but only while Skulled. Blessed Status players explicitly cannot equip the Amulet of Avarice. The asymmetry is by design: reduced risk means reduced ceiling.

This creates a natural space for an item that takes that philosophy even further. The Ring of Greed — a concept originally proposed by YouTuber PoisonedPotion — would double your drops within the Wilderness while Skulled.

The name "Ring of Greed" is a fitting counterpart to the Ring of Wealth. Where the Ring of Wealth passively improves your luck, the Ring of Greed would actively double your Wilderness loot — but only at the cost of the full Skulled risk. No safety net, no reduced fee, no tier buffers. The ring itself would be priced somewhere in the 1m–100m range, and is always lost on death.

PKers who already enjoy high risk high reward gameplay would find this far more appealing than Blessed Status ever could be. It gives the most committed, confident players a reason to stay in the Wilderness just as much as Blessed Status gives cautious players a reason to enter it. The progression reads naturally:

  • Blessed Status — Reduced risk, reduced reward ceiling. Good for PvMers, new players, and ironmen.
  • Unskulled — Normal risk and reward. The existing baseline.
  • Skulled + Amulet of Avarice — Full risk. Noted drops and a drop rate bonus at Revenants.
  • Skulled + Ring of Greed — Full risk. Double drops across the entire Wilderness.
Forinthry Gates & Forinthry Artefacts
Extended proposal — a parallel risk-vs-reward system for non-Blessed players

Blessed Status addresses the risk problem for players who want a safety net. But the same core problem — risk not feeling worth the reward, and players worldhopping constantly — also applies to unskulled and skulled PvMers who prefer not to use Blessed Status. Forinthry Gates and Forinthry Artefacts are a separate extended proposal that addresses that player bracket.

The short version: Forinthry Gates are 6 or so locations scattered through the Wilderness where you can convert accumulated loot into protectable Forinthry Artefacts — essentially insuring the GP you've earned in the Wilderness without having to leave it. They also let Tiered Blessed players log out or worldhop without losing their tiers.

Who Does This Help?
Benefits by player type
The Indiana Jones (PvMer)

Can engage with Wilderness PvM without risking everything. After a death, you lose 5% of your gear value — not 100% — so you can re-enter 20 times as often after a loss. You don't even have to rebuy gear on the GE; reclaim it from Death's Office. Wilderness farming also becomes viable: even if you die with 600k in Rev loot, you keep 450k of it. You can finally earn back your risk within the same trip.

The PKer

More players in the Wilderness means more targets. The death fee still pays out real GP via a Blessed Key on every Blessed kill, and a minimum 100k is always guaranteed — compare that to hunting 3-itemers carrying a spade. A less empty Wilderness is strictly better for PKers. Tiered Blessed Status also lets you see at a glance who has been in for a long time and is carrying more accumulated loot.

The New Player

Can learn Wilderness PvP and anti-PKing at a fraction of the cost. More importantly, they can learn it in the actual Wilderness against real players — not in a sanitised minigame that builds habits that don't transfer. Blessed Status players fighting each other creates a natural bracket of similarly inexperienced players to learn against, without being completely set back by a veteran every single time.

The Ironman

An ironman losing items in the Wilderness isn't losing GP — they're losing hours of farming time that can't be bought back on the GE. Blessed Status replaces that catastrophic loss with a manageable death coffer fee. Ironmen make up roughly 30% of the OSRS playerbase, and right now they have almost no reason to vote yes on any PvP poll. Blessed Status changes that directly.

The Clan

Saradomin alignment at the Forinthry Castle grants free Blessed Status to all clan members — enabling non-PvP clans to safely farm Wilderness World Bosses without extreme item risk.

Jagex

More Wilderness activity means more player retention, more bonds sold, more subscriptions renewed. The 100k activation cost functions as a gentle GP sink — sunk from the game whenever a Blessed player doesn't die to another player. Blessed Status doesn't require changing any existing weapon or armor stats, any loot tables, or any current Wilderness mechanics. It's a new overhead status that coexists alongside skulling and 3-iteming and can be rebalanced independently after release.

Why Would This Pass a Poll?
Addressing the core polling problem

Blessed Status is an additive, opt-in system. It does not remove anything from existing PKers. A PKer who never uses Blessed Status has their Wilderness experience entirely unchanged.

  • Non-PKers gain a reason to vote yes — they can now enter the Wilderness without dreading catastrophic loss.
  • Ironmen gain a reason to vote yes — finally a system that respects the time value of ironman items.
  • Existing PKers lose nothing — their risk, their rewards, their gameplay are completely intact.
  • The Wilderness gains more players — which is exactly what PKers have wanted for 10 years.
This is an expansion, not a replacement. Blessed Status does not make the Wilderness safer for PKers — it makes the Wilderness viable for the people who were never going to enter it otherwise. The pie gets bigger. Nobody's slice gets smaller.
