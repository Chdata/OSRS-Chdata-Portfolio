Removal of Trouver Parchments

Replace Trouver Parchments with tradeable item shards — eliminating GP generation concerns while making untradeables drop something meaningful.

The Problem
Why Jagex hasn't removed Trouver Parchments yet

After the recent Trouver Parchment rework, Jagex stated this in their news post:

Jagex — Gathering QoL, Trouver Changes & More "In an ideal world, we'd do away with Trouver Parchments entirely, but it's not safe for us to take this approach without risking more raw GP generation than we're comfortable with."

The concern is straightforward: if untradeables like Void Knight Equipment or the Fire Cape just drop freely on death in the Wilderness, that is effectively raw GP generation — the item has real value and can now be acquired without its original grind. Jagex does not want that. But this blocker has a clean solution.

The Solution — Item Shards
Remove GP generation from the equation entirely

Instead of an untradeable item dropping intact on death, it drops as tradeable broken or mangled shards of that item. The killer gets shards they can sell on the GE. The owner needs those same shards to repair their item. No raw GP enters the game at any point.

  • On death in the Wilderness, an untradeable item breaks and drops as tradeable Broken Equipment Shards (e.g. Broken Void Equipment Shard, Broken Fire Cape Shard). The (broken) item type already exists in the game.
  • If you die above Wilderness level 20, the item is instead mangled, dropping as more expensive Mangled Equipment Shards. The (mangled) item type also already exists in the game.
  • The NPC who originally sells the item also sells the shards at a fixed price — forcing the GE price to stay consistent and giving players a reliable fallback.
  • Players can repair their item by bringing the required shards back to the same NPC. Repairing can be free, or carry a small gold sink fee — designer's choice.
  • If a friend killed you, they can simply hand the shards back. If you killed yourself by mistake, same deal.
  • Trouver Parchments are not needed in this system at all. The shard economy replaces their function entirely.
No raw GP generation. The killer sells shards on the GE and gets taxed. The owner buys shards from the GE or the NPC. Every path is either a GP sink or GE-taxed — exactly what Jagex needs to feel comfortable removing Trouver Parchments.
How It Works in Practice
A concrete example

Say you're PKed in level 15 Wilderness while wearing Void Knight Equipment.

  • Your Void Knight Equipment breaks. The PKer receives a handful of tradeable Broken Void Equipment Shards.
  • The PKer can sell those shards on the GE. They have real value because other players need them to repair their own gear.
  • You go to the Void Knight NPC and buy Broken Void Equipment Shards at a set price — or buy them cheaper on the GE if supply is healthy.
  • You hand the shards to the NPC to repair your Void Knight Equipment. Done.

Now the same scenario but above level 20 Wilderness:

  • Your Void Knight Equipment is mangled. The PKer receives Mangled Void Equipment Shards — worth more, reflecting the deeper risk of deep Wilderness content.
  • Repairing from Mangled costs more shards or more expensive shards than repairing from Broken — creating a meaningful risk gradient between low and deep Wilderness.
This method gives untradeable items a real tradeable value in the Wilderness in a simple and straightforward way — without creating any item duplication, GP injection, or power creep. It is two new items per untradeable (Broken shard and Mangled shard), and the rest is just NPC shop entries and a death table change.
Transitioning Away from Trouver Parchments
What happens to existing parchments

Players who currently hold Trouver Parchments would not be left empty-handed:

  • Existing Trouver Parchments would be deleted from the game at the time of this update.
  • Players who held them would receive a free voucher redeemable for item shards of their choice — covering the value of the parchments they lost.
  • The Barrows equipment degradation system could potentially receive the same shard treatment, replacing the current GP-based repair cost with a more consistent shard economy.
This is something Jagex already wants to do. They said so themselves. This proposal simply gives them the mechanical path to do it safely. The blocker was always GP generation — item shards remove that blocker entirely.
