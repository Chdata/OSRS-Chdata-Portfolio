Improvements For Multiple PvP Minigames

Gear Disparity Problems & Minigame Hybrid Armor

Please give us Minigame Hybrid Armor again. Castle Wars is a minigame that suffers from being dominated by max level players with maxed equipment. Soul Wars is the same.

Additionally, if Jagex were to take my suggestions for the PvP Arena and Rookie Bounty Hunter seriously, we could reasonably expect that same exact problem to pop up.

It's not as fun for the casual, mid level, or the not-rich player when everyone else is just an unkillable war machine.

Seriously, Minigame Hybrid Armor is no longer a nostalgic wish of mine, it's a game balance necessity.

Oldschool Runescape now has all of the same armors in the game that were the reason Minigame Hybrid Armor was created for Pre-EoC in the first place.

Just as a quick reminder: Minigame Hybrid Armor was added to Runescape 2 after the addition of the Nex armors - Torva, Virtus, and Pernix.

In Runescape 2, each of those armors provided +40 max health and provided the best defensive and offensive capabilities of any other armor in the game.

And in Oldschool Runescape we literally have Torva and Virtus now, and we do technically also have Pernix... because Fortified Masori armor has nearly identical stats to what Pernix armor had in Runescape 2.

And damn, those guys in Masori are nearly impossible to kill or freeze. Meanwhile they hit constant 40s off-spec also.

Minigame Hybrid Armor essentially gave you the same stats as Nex armors, but without the +40 max health, and instead with a set effect providing a +15% damage bonus to the two relevant combat styles of that armor.

Please, for the love of the gods, throw us mid-level players a bone. It shouldn't cost billions of GP to be able to do well in a safe PvP minigame.

The Minigame Hybrid Armors were great for Pre-EoC minigame enjoyers. They don't affect the rest of the game including the main game economy or PvM and they don't affect the Wilderness.

They do add another reward incentive to play various different safe PvP minigames because you have to obtain the pieces of each set from different minigames. Please though, just don't make LMS a requirement to obtain these.

I personally would love to see the original Battlemage, Vanguard, and Trickster armor sets recreated in the Oldschool Runescape style. I always thought they looked absolutely sick, especially the Vanguard and Trickster armors.

As for the Battlemage armor, all I can say is... AMERICA

But just in-case people want to say "no, that looks too much like RS3", well, I have concepted my own version of Minigame Hybrid Armors so we could have a version unique to Oldschool Runescape.

Instead of a focus on the hybrid combat styles being mixed, I made a design focused more on the materials used to make them.

Let me introduce these 3 armor sets:

  • Lapis Lazuli Platerobes
  • Metal Dragonhide Armor
  • Dewleaf Armor

(I'll talk more about the concepts if they ever get seriously considered).

Minigame Crossbow, Bolts, & Arrows

As mentioned in my Castle Wars fixes page, the existing Castle Wars Bolts and Arrows could be rebranded to Minigame Ammunition and become available in other safe PvP minigames.

And we could use a "Fake Dragon Crossbow" or "Minigame Crossbow" to fire the Minigame Bolts.

Freeze Spam in Castle Wars and Soul Wars

In Pre-EoC the freeze spam was kind of balanced out because it costed you 1million gold per hour to use Ice Barrage in a safe PvP minigame with no monetary rewards. So very few people actually did that.

Nowadays anyone can do it for free in Castle Wars and in Soul Wars you can do it for 300k/h using Ancient Ice Sacks as opposed to 1.3m/h with runes.

Needless to say, it's a lot more prevalent than it ever was before in these minigames.

With that being the case, I think at least these minigames, which have the best potential to be a good casual forms of PvP, should offer some kind of options for negating freezes.

So like, maybe we can give both of these minigames an Anti-Freeze Potion.

You can just drink some antifreeze and get

  • Unfrozen with your normal duration of re-freeze immunity
  • A 1 minute cooldown before you can drink that potion again

The cooldown means that we won't completely invalidate freezing, thus requiring you to use it at the moment you really need it.

I think that would help these PvP minigames a LOT in terms of fun factor and just make them far less obnoxious of a freeze-fest.

The 1 minute cooldown is important so you can't just completely negate freezes when trying to break through the enemy's defense in Castle Wars.

Powered Staves in PvP Minigames

Most powered staves are already enabled in Castle Wars and Soul Wars. You can even use Tumeken's Shadow in these minigames if you want to.

Yet most powered staves are silly expensive to use in PvP minigames. Even the cheapest ones are still around 300k/h to use, to 500k/h, to 1m/h. These are expensive for newer and mid-level players looking to just have some casual fun.

I would love the return of a cheap, but strong powered stave like the Polypore Staff. I mean, we do have the Trident of the Seas, which is a direct copy paste of Polypore Staff, but it costs almost 9 times as much to use as Polypore Staff.

The whole point of Polypore Staff was that it made magic more financially accessible to more players, and I remember very distinctly back then it only costed around 60k per hour to use in charges. Trident of the Seas today costs 500k per hour.

How about for PvP minigames we just make our current powered staves cost 90% less to use by saving their charges 90% of the time within the minigames?

That would put most OSRS powered staves in line with the cost that Polypore Staff was per hour in Pre-EoC.

Or you know, since Ranged gets free bolts and arrows anyway, and Castle Wars gets a free rune pouch, why not just make powered staves completely free?

It shouldn't cost 1m per hour to play a safe PvP minigame.
