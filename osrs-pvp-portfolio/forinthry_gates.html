Forinthry Gates & Artefacts

A supplementary proposal that builds on Blessed Status — consolidate your PvM earnings in the Wilderness without leaving it.

Supplementary Proposal This page describes an extended idea that accompanies Blessed Status. Forinthry Gates & Forinthry Artefacts
An extended system for non-Blessed players — consolidate your risk without leaving the Wilderness

The ideas in the Blessed Status proposal focus heavily on Blessed Status players. But the same core problem — risk not feeling worth the reward, and players worldhopping constantly instead of staying engaged — also affects unskulled and skulled players doing PvM. Forinthry Gates address that without touching Blessed Status at all.

Originally conceived by Danil445, Forinthry Gates are 6 or so scattered locations throughout the Wilderness that serve two purposes: letting Tiered Blessed players log out or worldhop without losing their tiers, and letting non-Blessed players convert earned loot into protectable Forinthry Artefacts.

You cannot obtain Forinthry Artefacts while using Blessed Status. Forinthry Artefacts are explicitly a tool for unskulled and skulled players — continuing the pattern that higher risk options reward players proportionally more.
How Forinthry Gates Work
  • Some Forinthry Gates will have a red barrier — a PvP safe zone similar to Ferox Enclave. You cannot enter it while teleblocked.
  • Some Forinthry Gates will have no barrier — you must be out of combat to use these.
  • You can freely log out or worldhop at any Forinthry Gate without losing Tiered Blessed Status tiers.
  • You can convert loot into Forinthry Artefacts at any Forinthry Gate.

The result is that players are encouraged to make a decision each time they want to bank or escape: do you run for the edge of the Wilderness, or do you push further in to reach a Forinthry Gate and lock in your loot? It promotes sustained, organic gameplay rather than immediately worldhopping the moment anything happens.

Forinthry Artefacts
The one rule that matters: your most expensive Forinthry Artefact is always kept on death — a 5th protected item that sits on top of the items you'd normally keep and doesn't interfere with them. Everything else on this page is detail built on top of that single idea.

In three plain points:

  • What it is: your most expensive Forinthry Artefact is always protected on death, as an extra item that doesn't eat into your normal protected slots.
  • How you get one: turn loot you earned in the Wilderness into one at a Forinthry Gate, or convert an Ancient Artefact you found in the Wilderness.
  • Why you'd bother: it bundles your scattered Wilderness earnings into a single item you can protect, so getting caught doesn't wipe your whole trip.
Death WITHOUT an artefact
You lose all the loot from your inventory and looting bag. You lose the whole trip.
Death WITH an artefact
Merge your loot at a Forinthry Gate and it becomes a single Forinthry Emblem — which is protected on death no matter what.

Forinthry Artefacts mirror the Ancient Artefacts from Revenant Caves in structure and price. The difference is the protection above — an Ancient Artefact is always lost on death, while a Forinthry Artefact is the thing you keep. One concrete rule covers skulled play: if you die while skulled, the artefact splits and your killer gets half of it.

There is also a Forinthry Idol — worth 40m — obtained by combining two Forinthry Relics without ever leaving the Wilderness. It cannot be made from GE-bought Relics. Successfully creating it rewards you with a bonus 8m on top of its base value, reflecting the sustained risk of camping the Wilderness for the time it takes to accumulate two Relics.

How to Obtain Forinthry Artefacts
  • Method 1 — Ancient Artefact conversion: Convert an Ancient Artefact obtained within the Wilderness (cannot be bought on the GE) into its matching Forinthry Artefact at a Forinthry Gate. The prices match 1:1.
  • Method 2 — Looting Bag conversion: Use the left-click option on a Forinthry Gate to automatically destroy and convert alchables from your Looting Bag into a Forinthry Emblem. The Forinthry Gate pulls from a predetermined whitelist of items, and you may pay slightly more than the exact 550k price if the alchables in your bag don't add up to an exact amount. This method does not accept loot gained from killing players, and does not accept items brought into the Wilderness. The premium over the artefact's face value exists to account for the death protection — it should not function as a substitute for High Alchemy.
  • Upgrading: Sacrifice two Forinthry Artefacts of the same tier at a Forinthry Gate to merge them into the next tier. Both artefacts must have been obtained within the Wilderness without leaving.
Three points summarise the whole system: Forinthry Artefacts are a wealth consolidator that reduces your risk of losing GP earned in the Wilderness. They reward long, sustained trips. And they give 3-itemers and skullers a comparable tool to what Blessed Status gives PvMers — a way to reduce the sting of death without removing all risk.

Optional: the math, for optimisers

You never need any of this to use Forinthry Artefacts — "keep your most expensive one on death" is the whole feature. This section is only for players who want to compare expected loss across systems. These are averages across many deaths, not what happens on any single death.

  • Blessed Status: you keep roughly 75% of your earnings on death (you pay the 25% loot fee, plus ~5% on gear).
  • Unskulled with a Forinthry Artefact: you save roughly 50%–100% of your PvM earnings, depending on whether you die before reaching a gate (artefact saved, loot lost) or after consolidating at one (everything protected).
  • Skulled with a Forinthry Artefact: you save roughly 25%–50%, because the artefact splits in half on death and your unprotected gear is also at risk.

The pattern: the same "always protect the biggest artefact" rule produces a different effective saving depending on your skull state and whether you bank the value at a gate. Higher risk, higher reward — but you only ever interact with the concrete rules above, never the percentages.

The progression across all three systems flows naturally. A new player starts with Blessed Status. As they grow comfortable, they switch to unskulled play with Forinthry Artefacts to consolidate earnings. When confident enough to go all in, they reach for the Ring of Greed. Every step of the way, the Wilderness has something meaningful to offer them.
